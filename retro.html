Retro Games

What defines a retro game?

The distinction between retro and modern is heavily debated. Usually, retro gaming is based upon systems that are outmoded or discontinued. In this project, we will use time to define retro games, so any game 15 years or older qualifies as retro. Even so, we will showcase some games that go way beyond our 15 year mark.

Games we will be going over :

Donkey Kong Country (SNES)

Sonic the Hedgehog (SG)

K-ON! Ho-kago Live (PSP/PS3)

Q*bert (NES)

For this section we tried to choose games that are all unique and vary in their own ways, such as every game being an example of a different console. The mix also includes some very well known games, and games that may be not as well known today.

Game 1:

Donkey Kong Country:

Donkey Kong Country was inspired by the Super Mario series and was one of the first home console games to feature pre-rendered graphics, achieved through a compression technique that converted 3D models into SNES sprites with little loss of detail.

Mechanic 1

This game has various special animal friends that you can find, each with their own unique abilities.

Mechanic 2

You can play as either Donkey Kong or Diddy Kong, each character with subtle differences in gameplay.

Mechanic 3

Kong Country includes countless of secrets / secret rooms. Getting 100% on the game takes lots of exploring.

Game 2:

Sonic the Hedgehog:

Sonic the Hedgehog is a 1991 platform game developed by Sonic Team and published by Sega for the Genesis/Mega Drive. The game was designed for fast gameplay, and was influenced by games by the Mario creator, Shigeru Miyamoto.

Mechanic 1

Sonic has a large variety of powerups hiding around the maps, obtained by breaking the boxes they lie in.

Mechanic 2

When finishing the level with at least 50 rings, the game rewards you by bringing you to a special stage.

Mechanic 3

This game has many enemies with many styles of attacks, adding more challenge to each level you progress through.

Gallery

Game 3:

K-ON! Ho-kago Live:

K-ON! Ho-kago Live!! is a rhythm game on the Playstation Portable which was released by Sega on September 30, 2010. A HD remastered version for the PlayStation 3 was later released on June 21, 2012.

Mechanic 1

The game has a bonus clock feature, where you can choose your favourite character to read out the time to you.

Mechanic 2

You can unlock outfits to customize each member, which displays in the background visuals while playing.

Mechanic 3

The menu screen is very interactive, letting you unlock decorations,snacks, and secrets for the characters.

Game 4:

Q*bert:

Q*bert is an action game with puzzle elements played from an axonometric third-person perspective to convey a three-dimensional look. Many versions of Q*bert exist, do here we go over "Ultra" Q*bert for the NES.

Mechanic 1

Unique to this Q*bert game only, the 2 player mode gives us a green version of Q*bert!

Mechanic 2

Before starting every game, the game gives you the option to customize your controls.

Mechanic 3

There are multiple enemies; ones that chase, change back blocks, and move upside down.
